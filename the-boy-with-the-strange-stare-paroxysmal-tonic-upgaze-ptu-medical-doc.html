Since Landyn Withrow was born, his parents have known there is something wrong with him. He always looks up to the ceiling; has spells of vomiting, crying, stiffening up, and clenching his hands and feet; and has developmental delays. After going through several medical professionals, one doctor finally realises that Landyn has a rare neuromuscular disorder called Paroxysmal Tonic Upgaze (PTU). Plus, Cindy Webber has always been quite healthy, until a strange bruise appears on her right leg. This bruise comes and goes, but Cindy always has pain in that area. Just when she learns to deal with this symptom, she suddenly develops pain in her legs that soon spreads to her whole body and red dots start to appear on her right leg. It turns out that Cindy has a rare autoimmune disease called Microscopic Polyangiitis caused by vasculitis.

Every year, millions of Americans fall prey to real-life medical mysteries ailments that go undiagnosed or misdiagnosed for years. Their lives are thrown into turmoil as medical professionals struggle to understand their baffling conditions. Mystery diagnosis tells their stories. Through interviews and dramatic recreations, each medical mystery is recounted in vivid detail. Symptoms emerge, specialists are called in, but still the daily struggle continues. And then one day, because of a patients relentless pursuit of an accurate diagnosis, or a forward thinking doctor, the problem is solved and a true diagnosis given.
