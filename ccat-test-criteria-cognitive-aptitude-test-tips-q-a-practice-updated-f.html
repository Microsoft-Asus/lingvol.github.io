Arbel from JobTestPrep explains everything you need to know about the 2020 CCAT test, one of the most challenging pre-employment tests used by numerous companies worldwide.

The CCAT exam is 15 minutes long and consists of 50 questions covering 3 subjects: Spatial (Abstract) Reasoning, Math and Logic, Verbal.

The CCAT is a cognitive ability test – a test intended to measure your learning and problem-solving abilities. Scientific research has shown that practicing cognitive ability tests is effective only when simulating the actual task at hand.

Therefore, practicing the actual cognitive tasks of the CCAT, with the same test format and time limit, will have a major effect on your success.
