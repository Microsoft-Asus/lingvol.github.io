The first film in the "Trilogy of the Dollar" (1964) by Sergio Leone, starring Clint Eastwood, has given world fame to its director but also the composer of its soundtrack. And rightly so, because the music that the then 36-year old Ennio Morricone, despite initial hesitation of the director, has composed for "A Fistful of Dollars" is a creative stream still unmatched ...
The two soon found themselves in agreement on what issues and what effect it was to have the music on the film. According to Leone the ideal would be to start working to the music at the same time to write the script, to have a greater influence on the characterization of the scenes and characters.
Despite the international success of the soundtrack, which is associated with the entire western genre, Morricone not particularly appreciate; according to the composer, in fact, "A Fistful of Dollars" is the worst movie of Leone and the worst score I've ever done.
