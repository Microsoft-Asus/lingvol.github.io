What is this authorized capital? What does the term even mean?

The answer to that question is authorized capital is referred to as the value of shares, not the amount of money you invest. It is equal to the value of the shares. So, for instance, if you look at a company like Tata Motors or Reliance. They have one lakh shareholders; each share is worth say five hundred rupees. On the other hand, the amount of investment in the company may be much more than that. So when you talk about authorized capital, the authorized capital is only the value of the shares in the company. Coming to a small company or entrepreneur with a startup.
