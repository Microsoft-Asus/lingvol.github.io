Britain health authorities began rolling out the first doses of the widely-tested and independently reviewed COVID-19 vaccine on Tuesday, starting a global immunization program that is expected to gain momentum as more serums win approval. A 90-year-old woman received the first of two doses of the vaccine jointly developed by a US-based pharmaceutical giant Pfizer and Germany's BioNtech on Tuesday. Canada announced Monday he received its first doses of the same vaccine by the end of December. The Trump administration turned down the chance last summer to buy millions of additional doses of one of the leading Coronavirus vaccine contenders. That decision could delay the delivery of a second batch of doses until manufacturer Pfizer fulfills other international contracts. The revelation comes a day before President Trump aimed to take credit for the speedy development of forthcoming Coronavirus vaccines at a White House Summit being held later today. Pfizer's vaccine is expected to be endorsed by a panel of Food and Drug Administration advisors as soon as this week. US President-elect Joe Biden has reportedly chosen retired general Lloyd Austin to become a secretary Defense Secretary, that's according to a person familiar with the decision. Austin served under former President Barack Obama overseeing forces in the Middle East as head of the US Central Command. He would also be the first African American to run the Pentagon. The poll represents a tight battle between President Nana Akufo-Addo of the New Patriotic Party and his rival John Mahama of the National Democratic Congress party. The race marks the third time they have faced off against each other. Voters in Ghana are choosing between 12 presidential candidates and members of parliament for residents in 275 districts. Key issues among voters include education, health, unemployment and the country’s infrastructure. Election authorities are aiming to have the results of voting by later this evening.
Ethiopia’s government is denying that northern forces whom it forces have fought for a month would be able to mount a guerrilla insurgency. Federal troops have seized the federal, the regional capital from the former local ruling party, the Tigray People's Liberation Front (TPLF), and declared an end to their month-long offensive.
But the TPLF leaders say they are fighting back on various fronts. Ethiopia experts fear a drawn-out insurgency will have a (de)stabilizing impact around East Africa. The US House of Representatives voted Monday to welcome Hong Kong residents to live temporarily here in the US, vowing to be a beacon for rights as China clamps-down in the territory. The House moved by consensus to issue a so-called temporary protective status for five years to Hong Kong residents, meaning that people from the financial hub will have the right to work here in the United States and will not be subject to deportation. The initiatives still must be approved by the senate but it enjoys support across party lines. The United States on Monday place Nigeria on a religious freedom blacklist paving the way for potential sanctions if it does not approve improve its record. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo did not elaborate on the reasons for including Nigeria, which has a delicate balance between Muslims and Christians.
Other nations on the blacklist include Myanmar and North Korea. The United States said Monday that the legislative elections held in Venezuela on Sunday in which President Nicolas Maduro’s socialist political alliance is set to gain control of the National Assembly, were a sham. The National Assembly is the last of the country's branches of government where the US-backed opposition held sway.
