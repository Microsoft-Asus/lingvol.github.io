Official audio for Desmond's Dekker seminal version of Jimmy Cliff's anthemic song, which reached number 2 in the UK charts on 3 October 1970.

the recording's rhythm track was produced in Jamaica by leading hit-maker Leslie Kong, with vocal and horn overdubs arranged by British music legend, Vic Keary at his famed Chalk Farm studio in London.

The song provided the title for Desmond's best-selling album, released by Trojan Records soon after the record punctured the British charts on 22 August 1970.

it was also recorded that year by the singer's Trojan label-mate, Jimmy Cliff, whose version failed to chart, but featured in the Jamaican cult movie, "The Harder They Come", and later "Speed 2: Cruise Control", Will Smith's "Hitch" and the 2012 Aardman Animation film, "The Pirates! In an Adventure with Scientists!".

Desmond's more polished version was also used in the soundtrack of the 2010 British film, "Made in Dagenham" and more recently featured in the acclaimed documentary, "Rudeboy: The Story of Trojan Records" (from 2018).

Subsequent versions of the song include renderings by The Cimarons, Italian pop singer Sabrina (Sabrina Salerno), Stiff Little Fingers, French singer Johnny Hallyday and by Samuel E. Wright (as Sebastian the crab) in the motion picture, "The Little Mermaid".
