It all started the day I was born. Mom and Dad rushed to the hospital the morning I was born. My sister Louise stayed at home with my Grandma. Later in the afternoon, Grandma brought Louise to see me. She took one look at me and said,

“It's a boy!”

“Yes.” Smiled Mom and Dad. “Meet your baby brother Joe.”

“But I don’t want a baby brother!” Wailed Louise. “I want a baby sister!”

From that moment on she never let me forget that she wanted a baby sister. Whenever I tried to play with her, she would tell me to go away.

“You can’t play this game.” She would say. “This game is only for girls.”

Louise sometimes had her friends over to our house to play. They would go into her bedroom and shut the door so that I couldn’t come in to play. One time Louise was playing “tea party” with her friends. She had plates with cookies on and glasses of lemonade. I wanted to play too. I pushed the door open so hard that I tumbled into the room and fell on top of the plates and glasses. Louise started shouting and crying. Mom came running upstairs.

“Oh what have you done Joe?” She asked.

“He’s ruining everything Mom.” cried Louise.

With that Mom picked me up and took me downstairs to sit in the room with her. It wasn’t fair. I told Mom it was only an accident but she didn’t believe me.

Sometimes Mom would ask Louise to play with me whilst she made dinner. I was so happy. But Louise didn’t speak to me. She just put the TV on and ignored me.

Louise had lots of friends because she went to a big school with lots of students. I couldn’t wait to start school. Then I would get lots of friends too. Finally the day came. My first day at school.
