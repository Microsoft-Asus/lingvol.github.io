Anarchism is a socio-political model taking various forms, but typically upholding certain specific principles. Broadly speaking, an anarchist society is a non-coercive anti-hierarchical society achieved without the traditional state apparatus in which members choose voluntarily to participate.

This video provides a taxonomy of anarchism, explaining the primary features of different anarchist models. These descriptions are based on mainstream scholarly literature (see the bibliography link below), and can be considered reliably accurate in broad brush stroke terms.
