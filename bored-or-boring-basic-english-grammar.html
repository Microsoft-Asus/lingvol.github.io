In this lesson, learn how to use "to be bored" and " to be boring" and improve your basic English grammar.

It's a common English mistake to confuse "bored" and "boring".

But grammatically they aren't interchangeable.

We use "bored" and "boring" in very specific situations.

"Bored" is used to describe the feeling or emotion.

"Boring" is used to describe what causes the feeling or emotion of boredom.

By the end of the lesson, you'll feel confident using "bored" and "boring" in grammatically correct speech.
