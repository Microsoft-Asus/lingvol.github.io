--- title: "WisEye Morning Minute: State Approved for Additional $300 Weekly UI Benefit" image: "https:\/\/i.ytimg.com\/vi\/rhWcXtIbJs0\/hqdefault.jpg" vid_id: "rhWcXtIbJs0" categories: "News-Politics" tags: ["WisconsinEye","WisEye","Rewind: Your Week in Review"] date: "2020-12-12T09:08:02+03:00" vid_date: "2020-09-08T13:29:15Z" duration: "PT1M52S" viewcount: "196" likeCount: "1" dislikeCount: "0" channel: "WisconsinEye" --- {% raw %}During Rewind: Your Week in Review, WisconsinEye senior producer Steve Walters and WisPolitics.com editor JR Ross discussed Governor Tony Evers joining other states in applying for an additional jobless benefit of $300 as ordered by President Donald Trump. The additional $300 would be added to the state weekly maximum UI benefit of $370. The first additional federal benefit of $600 per week ended in July, and no date is set for the first $300 additional benefits to start. "The state got approved for three weeks of this benefit," Ross said. "I was told by DWD it was encouraged to apply for a fourth week. It will take, however, six to eight weeks...to get the money, so it's not going to show up anytime soon." Ross added that under the program, there is $44 billion under FEMA to pay for these extended benefits. The U.S. House may still bargain to have some payments retroactive after that $600 additional benefit expired in July. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi would like to go back to the $600 additional benefit.
