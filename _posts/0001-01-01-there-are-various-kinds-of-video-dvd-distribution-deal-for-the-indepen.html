Hollywood lives off of Treatments and Scripts; Ideas are a dime-a-dozen and you believe you have a Great Idea...so to bring it into the system and hopefully make money while practicing your Oscar® acceptance speech, you must take your Idea, that has no value, but might be Great, and make it into a tangible form that you can likely protect... And that tangible for is called a Treatment.

Write a 3-5 Page Treatment...So get 3-5 pieces of 8.5 x 11 blank paper.

PAGE-1 is ACT I: Double spaced typing. Allocate the Top Half of Page 1 and type the 5 "Ws & H" (Who, What, Where, When, Why & How). And each time, in the treatment that you introduce a NEW CHARACTER type his/her name in all UPPER CASE Letters, then give his/her age and a 7-15 word visualization of the person.

Then, as the story continues in Act I, Act II & Act III each time you type the character again, if you do, just use Upper & Lower case letters.

PAGE-2+ (Half of Page-1) & (Half of Page-3) is ACT II: Know the Roller Coaster Ride (5 UH-OHS, 5 OH-SHITS & 1 OH-MY-GOD) that your primary story (you're A-Story) takes and use these 11 Scenes as one paragraph each of prose typing and, again, each time you introduce a new character, all UPPER CASE Letters, Age & description... This will probably, space wise, entail the Bottom-Half of Page-1, all of Page-2, and the Top-Half of Page-3.

PAGE-3 is ACT III: Type how your movie ends with you're a-Story coming to a conclusion as the 3 Sub-Plots (B-Stories) resolve themselves and, in so doing, become intertwined into the A-Story and pushes the A-Story to resolve itself.

COVER PAGE: Centered, 6 lines down, type the (A) TITLE. Skip a line and centered type (B), within parenthesis, "A Treatment for Feature Film". Then skip a line and centered (C) type the small case word "by". Then skip a line and (D) type "your name". Finally, in the bottom left hand corner, single space typing in all capital letters (E) type "CONTACT DETAILS and then type (F) ADDRESS, PHONE & E-Mail... Don't hide.

TREATMENT: 3-5 Pages, with a proper cover page, a Beginning, a Middle & an End, with 5-7 fleshed out characters and a solid Roller Coaster A-Story with a skeleton structure of 11 Scenes.

REGISTER: Yes! Why? Because you can't copyright a Treatment, however, you still need proof-of-creation, that has merit in our court system, on the specific day you created this thing. And, the Writers Guild of America states that a Treatment IS A FINISHED PRODUCT and will gladly accept your $20 (non-members) and $10 (members) and give you a receipt that verifies the Date They Received It.

POORMAN COPYRIGHT: Don't do it. Don't do it. Don't do it. WHY? Because it has absolutely no merit in the court system. Don't do it. Don't do it. In another post (Hot Tip) I'll tell you why the Poorman's Copyright is useless.
