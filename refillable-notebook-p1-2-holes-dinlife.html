Informations about the things I've used:

(This Hemp fabric is come from H'Mong people in Vietnam, one not dyed, one dyed with nature Terminalia catappa leaves)
A steel wire (about 1mm thick) is 4 times the height of the notebook. You can find the details of how I bended the wire in the end of this video: https://youtu.be/CL9PIjZF9Js

Basic stitching techniques: https: / /youtu.be/XRfuHlR7ImM
----------------------------------

I have started making notebooks like this since about the year 2010, when a sister gave me a paper punch tool. And much later I discovered that I can replace the papers inside, so I was able to write lots of papers with just one notebook. The old papers are then tied neatly or recycling.

If you don't have Paper Punch tool, you can watch my next video about making Stitches Notebook with reusable cover (https://youtu.be/tpC79FmXZbM)

And like I always said, I hope you can use old clothes or fabric grown organic and dyed naturally.
