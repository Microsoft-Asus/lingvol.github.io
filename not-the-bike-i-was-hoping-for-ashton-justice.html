So, hopefully you've seen both the build video and the first riding video of this Ashton Justice (if not check them out in the links below) so you'll know that I had one of these (or at least a prototype) back in the 2000s and absolutely loved it and had some good competition results.

I must admit I've not enjoyed riding it as much as I had hoped, first I think the lightweight spec was too stretched out and now with some parts closer to how I had mine it's still not riding how I remember...I think I might have had some rose tinted glasses on!

Don't get me wrong I'm happy with the riding I've done, I think I looked good on it but I never felt comfortable and things I'd consider easy ended up taking multiple attempts to film...in fact this video has taken longer to make than most videos on my channel, I've tried to film on 7 separate days (a normal video takes one or two) and I've either had to give up due to rain, being too busy or just getting frustrated and giving up.

I feel the bike is not as good at street as my Hex, the lack of being able to bunnyhop and relying on techniques I've not used for over a decade meant I was limited with the height of walls I could get up and the weight means it's going to be a bit sluggish on natural (although better than the Hex due to the longer wheel base) where a more modern lighter bike would be better.

I still love the design though and would definitely keep hold of it if it was mine but it would need to lose a kilo or 4 or have a bit of a higher bb for it to keep up with modern bikes.

Despite my difficulties what did you think?
