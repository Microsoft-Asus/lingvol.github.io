White House says Bolton's book can't be published due to "significant amount" of classified information

Let's start our international news in Washington...
The White House says the upcoming book by President Trump's former top national security aide John Bolton can't be published.
For more on this and other news around the world, let's turn to our Yoon Jung-min.
Jung-min, this face-off between Bolton and his former boss is boiling over as the impeachment trial rumbles on in the U.S. Senate.
Right, Conn-young. The White House's National Security Council says Bolton's unpublished book contains "significant amounts" of classified information, so it cannot be published.
This comes as Democrats are pressing to summon the former National Security Advisor for testimony in the impeachment trial.
The NSC said in a letter to Bolton's lawyer that the manuscript may not be disclosed without removing the classified information, mentioning Bolton's duty to stick to non-disclosure agreements.
Bolton's attorney Charles Cooper says the letter was dated January 23rd.
The Washington Post reports that Cooper denied the White House's claims,... saying the book does not contain anything classified.
The attorney added his client is currently preparing for the possibility that the Senate might subpoena him to testify.
Earlier, the New York Times published details of the book,... sparking a frenzy on Capitol Hill.
The manuscript reportedly says President Trump told Bolton in August that he wanted to freeze 391-million dollars of aid to Ukraine until the country helped him investigate former Vice President Joe Biden and his son over allegations of corruption.
Following the New York Times report, President Trump took to Twitter, saying he never told Bolton such things and called the book "nasty and untrue."
Bolton's book, "The Room Where It Happened: A White House Memoir," is slated to go on sale on March 17th.
