Salote, aged 25, initially found her tuberculosis (TB) treatment difficult to manage and felt isolated and alone. Fearful of facing stigma, she distanced herself from her neighbours. But daily visits from community-based health promoters have provided a vital lifeline. UNDP and the Global Fund, through the Ministry of Health, are partnering with volunteers from the Tuvalu Red Cross to ensure people are supported during the often gruelling, but life-saving, treatment regime.
