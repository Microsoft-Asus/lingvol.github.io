In focus today: "Wait For Me Daddy", 1940
This is a famous historical picture, better known as "Wait for Me, Daddy", taken in October 1st, 1940, in Canada.

Canadian photographer, Claude Dettloff, was getting ready to take the picture of the whole column of the British Columbia Regiment, marching down the hill in New Westminster. They were being deployed and families were about to be separated by the War. A five-year-old, Warren "Whitey" Bernard, suddenly ran out of his mother's grasp to his father, Private Jack Bernard, for the last goodbye. And history was made when the photographer snapped the shot.

The mother's outstretched hand and the swirl of her coat, the boy's shock of white hair and his own reaching hand, the father's turning smile and the downward thrust of his own outreaching hand, the long line of marching men in the background, all this makes an unforgettable image, a masterpiece of unplanned composition, a heart-grabbing moment, frozen for all time. The picture was quickly picked up all over the world, getting exposure in LIFE magazine and it hung in every school in British Columbia during the war.

Jack Bernard survived the war and came back home, after 5 years, in October 1945.

"The day Dad came home, my grandfather picked me up and took me to the station. That was probably the happiest day in my 10-year-old life", Warren said in an interview.

On October 4th, 2014, the city of New Westminster unveiled a bronze statue honouring the photo. At the unveiling of the statue, everyone was overwhelmed by the emotion this photograph evokes.
